Beef Pares

Beef Pares is a sweet and savory dish in the Philippines. The distinct flavor comes from the beef braised in Star Anise and other local aromatics. It usually is an extension of Bulalo as it is almost the cooked in the same method by cooking the clear Bulalo broth then taking a part of it to be used as a base for the Pares.

Pares is also got it name because it "pairs" well with rice or mami noodles

Ingredients

  • Beef Cubes
  • Roughly Chopped Garlic
  • Brown Sugar
  • Soy Sauce
  • Star Anise
  • Finely Chopped Ginger
  • Rice
  • Beef Knorr Cubes
  • Corn Starch
  • Hoisin Sauce (optional)

Steps

  1. Wash your rice in a bowl with clean water and strain it. Repeat 2 times. Cook in a rice cooker
  2. Saute Garlic and Ginger together until aromatic
  3. Add the beef and let it sear until brown
  4. Turn your heat to medium low. Add Brown Sugar and Soy Sauce into the pan and let it caramelize. Make sure to keep moving the beef around as to not burn the sugars.
  5. Add water until the beef is covered. Add Star Anise and Beef Knorr Cubes.
  6. Increase heat to bring it to a boil then lower heat until it is a gentle simmer. Let it simmer for 20-40 minutes depending on your preferences. Make sure to keep an eye on the beef and refill with water it evaporates too much.
  7. You can optinally add the hoisin sauce at this stage. You can also adjust soy sauce and brown sugar to taste.
  8. Mix corn starch and water to make a slurry and add to the beef pares. You can adjust to your preference on how thick you want your sauce to be. The sauce will also be thicker once it cools down.
  9. Serve beef pares over cooked rice.
