Pork Kare-Kare

Kare-Kare is a peanut butter based Filipino Stew that is usually served with Bagoong to balance out the fatiness of the peanut butter and fatty meat

Today we are making a take on Kare-kare where we prepare our Pork with chashu-style technique from Japanese cuisine.

Ingredients

  • Pork Shoulder (rolled into a log)
  • Roughly Chopped Garlic
  • Whole Garlic
  • Sliced White Onions
  • Roughly Chopped White Onions
  • Roughly Chopped Ginger
  • Whole black peppercorns
  • Bay Leaves
  • Rice
  • Pork Knorr Cubes
  • Peanut Butter
  • Bagoong
  • String Beans
  • Eggplant
  • Pechay or Bok Choy
  • Neutral Cooking Oil.
  • Corn Starch
  • Annatto seeds (optional)

Steps

  1. Wash your rice in a bowl with clean water and strain it. Repeat 2 times. Cook in a rice cooker
  2. Roll your pork shoulder into a log and tie with Cooking Twine or Butcher's Twine. You can also buy and ask the butcher to roll the pork shoulder into a log for you already.
  3. Sear the pork shoulder roll on all sides including the ends. Just sear enough so that a golden crust forms.
  4. Put the pork shoulder into a large pot and cover with water. Add bay leaves, ginger, whole garlic, roughly chopped white onions, black peppercorns as your aromatics. Add Pork Knorr cubes. Bring to a boil and let it simmer for 2 hours. Skim off any scum that forms on the top. Refill with water if it evaporates too much.
  5. Once pork is well cooked, remove from pot and set aside. Let it cool and put in the fridge and continue cooling for 20-30 minutes or until when you are ready to serve.
  6. Strain the stock that was used to cook the pork shoulder in and set aside. In a new pot, saute the chopped garlic and chopped onions with cooking oil until fragrant.
  7. Add your pork stock into the sauted onions and garlic. Add Peanut butter to the mixture.
  8. Optionally, set aside submerged annatto seeds in warm water. Staing the water after it has developed color and add to your kare-kare pot.
  9. Add your eggplant, string beans and pechay to the mixture and cook only for 5-10 minutes. This is to keep the vegetables integrity but cook it enough to be easy to eat.
  10. Get your pork shoulder log and slice thin pieces of it from the log. Torch lightly on both sides. Alternatively, you may also pan fry the meat to your liking.
  11. Serve the pork shoulder with the kare-kare sauce with vegetables, rice and bagoong.
